Chapter 3

Humanity's Self-awareness Process and the Dominance of the Linear Model

This chapter explores how humanity developed a linear model of the universe through self-awareness, which created a false perception of disconnection from the universe's self-organizing process.

The Development of Linear Perception

Humans used hands and vocal systems to increase cooperation. Sign language and vocal projections created abstract communication, enabling discussion of distant objects. This subject-object separation was a major achievement in consciousness development but also created an illusion of separation from the environment.

Stable, orderly observations of natural cycles led to belief in a linear cause-and-effect model. This reductionist approach assumes "the whole is equal to the sum of its parts," which approximates but overlooks nonlinear reality operating "at the edge of chaos." In truly nonlinear systems, "cause and effect are not proportional," and outcomes emerge from complex web interactions rather than single dominant causes.

Linear Thinking as Survival Mechanism

Linear thinking functions as a survival mechanism, activating brain regions associated with procedural memory and defensive responses. This defensive orientation creates negative perception biases: fear, guilt, blame, anger, violence, and hopelessness. The bias toward threat assessment reduces information processing quality, particularly regarding chaotic events.

Linear relationships emerged based on self-assertion, short-term gain, domination, competition, control, and liability management, creating values emphasizing rigidity, quantity, and consumption.

Brain Hemispheres

According to neuroscientists Kelly McGonigal, Iain McGilchrist, and Jill Bolte Taylor, the left hemisphere processes information analytically, deterministically, and judgmentally in linear fashion, while the right operates nonlinearly, holistically, and spiritually. Linear thinking and left-side brain activity is dominant not only in our minds, but throughout our societal structures.

The "default state of mind" involves critical judgment of the present, rumination about past and future, and creation of an ego-driven identity requiring defense. This prevents full attention to present-moment challenges.

Systems Thinking — The Alternative

Systems thinking recognizes the universe as a highly interconnected, dynamic network of relationships where components interact to produce emergent processes. It is a holistic logic based on behaviors of nonlinear living networks in which all members implement their assets in a manner of cooperation and partnership. This thinking welcomes change and challenges as it recognizes chaos as a prerequisite to evolve to a higher level of cognitive order.

Shifting from linear to systems thinking transforms perception from separation to connectedness. Emotional patterns shift from negativity, blame, anger, revenge, violence, and hopelessness to logic that is observant, positive, forgiving, tolerant, peaceful, and hopeful.

Values transform: consumption to conservation, competition to cooperation, quantity to quality, domination to partnership, rigidity to flexibility, and liability management to asset management.

Mindfulness and the Zone

Systems thinking enables mindfulness, a state where events are experienced through senses rather than judgment. Athletes and musicians describe this as being "in the zone" where intuitive instruction guides nearly effortless performance.

The goal is unification of linear and nonlinear perception in order to achieve the ultimate potential of our creative minds. Linear modeling and reductionist science are incredibly useful tools that enabled understanding of nonlinear science itself.

The Body as Network

The body exemplifies nonlinear network principles. The brain, though only 2.5% of body weight, dominantly controls the system and consumes more energy per gram per second than other organs, making it the hub.

However, linear thinking illusively separates brain from body, creating relationships of self-assertiveness and short-term gain rather than holistic integration.

The body comprises interconnected networks: respiratory, circulatory, immune, nervous, muscular, skeletal — all cognitive systems. The heart perceives through gut feeling and intuition beyond mechanical function.

Observing actions, thinking, and habits systemically activates self-organizing feedback. This conscious participation allows synchronization of left and right brain hemispheres, eventually syncing whole brain with whole body network.

The paradigm shift will start within our individual body networks, and will expand to any network that we are participating in at any scale.
